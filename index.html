About Me

I grew up in Louisiana so I have an instinctual love for cajun food. One of my favorites is boudin, pronounced "BOO-dan", a spicy pork, rice and vegtable mixture stuffed into a sausage casing. The links are easy to eat and great for breakfast or a late night snack. It's definately cajun comfort food and every small town between Beaumont and New Orleans will have boudin.

I'm half Korean so I naturally love Korean food, especially Korean BBQ. What I love most about Korean BBQ, besides the marinated meats grilled at the table, are the small side dishes of vegetables called Banchan. They come out at the start of the meal and give you something to much on before the main course. Marinated cucumbers, bean sprouts, and seaweed salad are some examples and most people eat them with a bit of steamed rice that normally comes out with the banchan. There are many more types of banchan and each restaurant may be different depending on what vegetables are in season.

After high school I joined the Navy and was stationed in Pearl Harbor, Hawaii. In my free time I learned to surf and went to college at Hawaii Pacific University where I earned a bachelors degree in Business Administration. One of my computer courses taught me about databases and that got me interested in Information Technology.

After the military and college I found my way to Austin, Texas and started a career in Information Technology. My first job in this new career was as a Systems Support Specialist for the Texas Department of Human Services. I learned how to make websites dynamic with data from databases using Cold Fusion and SQL Server. Then I moved on to Austin Community College where I developped a web-based budget development system for the college to allocate funds to each department for the upcoming year. It was built with Active Server Pages and SQL Server. I eventually moved to a Database administrator position at the college before I moved on to the private sector.

My career at Visa started as a contractor supporting Verified by Visa. After about 10 months I converted to a full time position and have been at Visa ever since. I have been at Visa for over 15 years and have been a Programmer Analyst, Systems Engineer, Manager, Director and currently a Sr. Director of Product Reliability Engineering. Visa is a great place to work and I am very proud to be a part of it.
